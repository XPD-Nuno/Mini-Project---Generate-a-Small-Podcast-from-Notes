Title: David Attenborough turns 100 and gets a newly identified species named after him

Summary: Scientists have named a newly identified species of tiny parasitic wasp after David Attenborough to honor his 100th birthday. The wasp is called Attenboroughnculus tau and was discovered in southern Chile before being stored in the collection of London’s Natural History Museum for more than 40 years.

Researchers later confirmed that the specimen was not only a new species, but also belonged to a newly identified genus. The discovery highlights how many species remain unnamed in museum collections and why taxonomy is important for understanding and protecting biodiversity.

David Attenborough has inspired generations of scientists through his documentaries, books, and long career in natural history broadcasting. More than 50 species have already been named after him, and this new discovery is another tribute to his impact on science and conservation.
